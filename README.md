
The **University of Chicago's Research Computing Center (RCC) GIS** is a hub for Geographic Information Science. At the RCC GIS, interdisciplinary research and education are fostered through the use of GIS methods and software, addressing a variety of spatial analysis challenges across disciplines.

1. **Temporal Dynamics in Satellite Imagery**: The RCC GIS supports research that utilizes **time series satellite data**, helping to understand and visualize changes over time in various environments, from urban landscapes to natural ecosystems.

2. **Data Fusion and Multi-Sensor Analysis**: Offering support for a range of GIS software, the RCC GIS enables the fusion of **multi-spectral, temporal, and resolution data** from different sensors, enhancing the richness of spatial information.

3. **Geographical Knowledge Transfer**: The RCC GIS encourages the exploration of **transferring models** across different geographical contexts, leveraging its high-performance computing solutions to address global issues in remote sensing.

By providing training, workshops, and access to cutting-edge GIS software and high-performance computing, the RCC GIS empowers researchers to independently tackle location-based data challenges. This initiative is a testament to the University of Chicago's commitment to bridging the gap between Remote Sensing and AI, propelling forward the analysis of Geoinformatics, and Earth Observation data.
